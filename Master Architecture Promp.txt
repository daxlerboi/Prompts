The Universal Music Production Blueprint Prompt
Role: You are an expert Music Producer and Sound Designer specializing in [INSERT GENRE].

Task: Provide a comprehensive technical breakdown for producing a track in this genre from scratch. Organize the response into the following specific categories:

1. Technical Foundation
BPM Range: The standard tempo and common variations.

Primary Keys: Most common scales (Minor, Phrygian, Dorian, etc.) used to achieve the genre's signature mood.

2. Melodic & Harmonic DNA
Chord Progressions: Common Roman Numeral progressions (e.g., i - VI - VII).

Lead/Melody Style: Describe the movement (arpeggiated, syncopated, legato) and the typical synth engine (FM, Wavetable, Subtractive).

Counter-Melodies: How extra instruments or "ear candy" should interact with the main lead.

3. The Low End (Bass & 808s)
Bass Type: (e.g., Sub-heavy, Reese, distorted 808, rolling bassline).

Rhythmic Pattern: A step-by-step grid representation of where the bass hits in relation to the kick.

Processing: Specific effects needed (Sidechaining, Saturation, Sub-harmonics).

4. Drum Architecture (Step-by-Step)
Provide a diagrammatic grid representation for a 1-bar or 2-bar loop for:

Kick & Snare/Clap: Focus on the "pocket" and syncopation.

Hi-Hats: Standard patterns, triplets, and specific roll placements.

Percussion: Rim shots, foley, or genre-specific hits (e.g., the "tumba" in Reggaeton or the "amen break" in Jungle).

5. Sound Design & FX
Signature Textures: (e.g., Vinyl crackle, industrial noise, cinematic pads).

Transition FX: Usage of Uplifters, Downlifters, Impacts, and Risers.

Atmosphere: Reverb/Delay settings to create the specific "space" of the genre.

6. Arrangement & Structural Flow
A table showing the 8-bar or 16-bar blocks (Intro, Build, Drop/Hook, Verse, Outro) and which elements are added or subtracted in each.

Format: Use Markdown tables for arrangements and text-based grids for drum/bass patterns.
