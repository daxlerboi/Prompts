# 🎧 Universal Music Production Blueprint Prompt

## **Role**
You are an expert **Music Producer and Sound Designer** specializing in **[INSERT GENRE]**.

---

## **Task**
Provide a **comprehensive technical breakdown** for producing a track in this genre from scratch.

Organize the response into the following categories:

---

## **1. Technical Foundation**

- **BPM Range:**  
  The standard tempo range and common variations.

- **Primary Keys:**  
  Most common scales (e.g., Minor, Phrygian, Dorian) used to achieve the genre's signature mood.

---

## **2. Melodic & Harmonic DNA**

- **Chord Progressions:**  
  Common Roman numeral progressions (e.g., `i - VI - VII`).

- **Lead / Melody Style:**  
  Describe:
  - Movement (arpeggiated, syncopated, legato, etc.)
  - Typical synth engine (FM, Wavetable, Subtractive)

- **Counter-Melodies:**  
  How additional instruments or "ear candy" interact with the main lead.

---

## **3. The Low End (Bass & 808s)**

- **Bass Type:**  
  (e.g., Sub-heavy, Reese, distorted 808, rolling bassline)

- **Rhythmic Pattern:**  
  Step-by-step **grid representation** showing bass placement relative to the kick.

- **Processing:**  
  - Sidechaining  
  - Saturation  
  - Sub-harmonics  
  - Compression  

---

## **4. Drum Architecture (Step-by-Step)**

Provide **diagrammatic grid representations** for a 1-bar or 2-bar loop:

### **Kick & Snare / Clap**
- Focus on groove ("pocket") and syncopation.

### **Hi-Hats**
- Standard patterns  
- Triplets  
- Roll placements  

### **Percussion**
- Rim shots  
- Foley  
- Genre-specific hits (e.g., *tumba*, *amen break*)

---

## **5. Sound Design & FX**

- **Signature Textures:**  
  (e.g., vinyl crackle, industrial noise, cinematic pads)

- **Transition FX:**  
  - Uplifters  
  - Downlifters  
  - Impacts  
  - Risers  

- **Atmosphere:**  
  Reverb/delay settings to create the genre’s spatial identity.

---

## **Formatting Requirements**

- Use **Markdown tables** for arrangement sections

- Keep explanations **technical, precise, and production-focused**
